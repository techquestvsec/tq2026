Beat the Beats

Where technology meets rhythm—experience the future of sound.

Software: FL Studio
Mode: Individual
Open To: Classes 6–12
Categories: Junior (6-8) & Senior (9-12)
Max Participants: 12
Duration: 2 Hours
Venue: Senior Computer Lab
Overview

Participants mix and remix provided audio tracks using FL Studio to create an original, high-impact musical arrangement. Bring your own wired headphones or earphones — wireless are not permitted. Participants without headphones will be disqualified.

Pre-Event Selection
  • When: 2 weeks before the event.
  • Format: Prepare a beat using a provided sample pack, or prepare a piano rack — within the given time.
  • Duration: 40 minutes — 2 tasks.
  • Judging Basis: Creativity + Rhythm + Smooth Sound/Beats.
  • Who Proceeds: Top 12 participants advance to the main event.
Event Structure

Round 1 — SoundCheck Showdown (40 minutes)

The opening round where participants put their core mixing skills to the test. Contestants are given raw audio tracks and must balance, enhance, and creatively mix them within a limited time. This round focuses on fundamentals like EQ, levels, and transitions. Only those who can turn basic sound into a polished mix move forward.


Round 2 (Finale) — Final Frequency Showdown (1 hour 20 minutes)

The ultimate battle where creativity meets mastery. Participants push beyond basics to craft a unique, high-impact mix that showcases their signature style. With complex tracks, advanced effects, and complete creative freedom, this round tests innovation, precision, and stage presence. Only the most skilled sound architects will rise above the noise and claim the crown.

Rules
  • Participants must bring their own wired headphones — participants without headphones will be disqualified.
  • Only FL Studio may be used.
  • Use of all packs available within FL Studio is permitted.
  • Use of inbuilt software tools is permitted.
  • Internet access is not allowed.
  • All mixes must be created during the event — pre-made or pre-edited tracks are not allowed.
  • Only the provided audio samples / tracks (or approved resources) can be used.
  • Time limits are strict — late submissions will not be accepted.
  • Avoid distortion, clipping, or poor balancing — these may lead to deductions.
  • Final mixes must be submitted to the event host on the spot. File name format: Name, Class and Section.
  • Maintain professionalism and respect towards judges and fellow participants.
  • The judges’ decisions will be final and binding in all cases.
Judging Criteria
  • Technical Precision (50 marks per round) — Accuracy in mixing: balance, EQ, clarity, transitions, and overall sound quality.
  • Creativity & Originality — How uniquely participants blend tracks, use effects, and create a distinct sound.
  • Impact & Presentation — The overall vibe, energy, and how engaging or powerful the final mix feels.

Marks Breakdown: 50 marks per round (Round 1 + Final Round) = 100 marks total (including bonuses and deductions). Deductions of −5 to −10 for distortion, poor transitions, or rule violations.

Tiebreaker — “Sudden Sound Clash”: In case of a tie, finalists are given a surprise audio sample or genre on the spot and 10–15 minutes to create a quick mix. The participant who delivers the most creative, clean, and impactful mix under pressure wins.
