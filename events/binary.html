Logic Solving

Diffuse the bomb

Detect. Decode. Diffuse.

Mode: Individual
Format: Computer Based
Duration: 1 Hour
Open To: Classes 5–10
Categories: Junior (5–7) & Senior (8–10)
Max Participants: Top 10 per category
Venue: Junior Computer Lab
Overview

20 finalists, 2 categories, 1 ticking clock. Top qualifiers from Juniors and Seniors compete in a live, multi-layered digital escape challenge to see who can crack the system first. Participants complete a selection round 2–3 weeks after registration; the top 10 from each category advance to the Final Showdown.

Selection Round
  • When: 2–3 weeks after registration closes.
  • Format: Solve a given puzzle within a limited time.
  • Duration: 1 hour — judged on speed & accuracy.
  • Who advances: Top 10 participants from each category.
Final Showdown
  • Duration: 1 hour on computer.
  • All 20 finalists compete simultaneously in a live, multi-layered digital escape challenge.
  • Pen & paper will be provided at the lab for working calculations.
Judging & Scoring
  • Technical Skill (Shortcuts / Navigation) — 10 pts
  • Problem Solving (Logic / Independence) — 10 pts
  • Accuracy (Correct Codes / Flags) — 10 pts
  • System Ethics (Staying in bounds) — 10 pts
  • Speed (Time of completion) — 10 pts
  • Total: 50 points. Tiebreaker: speed; if still tied, the participant with the higher Problem Solving score (fewest hints used) wins.
Rules
  • Hints: Two hints are free per participant. The first hint costs 5 minutes; the second costs 10 minutes.
  • Pen & paper allowed for solving — provided at the lab.
  • No internet access permitted.
  • No external hardware allowed.
  • Any communication between competitors results in immediate disqualification.
  • Modifying or deleting OS settings is not allowed.
  • Raise your hand if your timer stops; report any technical glitch to the judges instantly.
  • Submission method: Paper.
