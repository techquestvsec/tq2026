Students choose their own topic, get it approved, then have a month to build anything they can imagine — and pitch it live on event day.

OpenCanvas is TechQuest's most unrestricted event. No fixed guidelines, no required output, no wrong answers. A student who picks "A Tree" might build a family history explorer, a breathing ecosystem, a puzzle game, or an art piece. Every interpretation is valid.

Two stages:

The Build

Phase 01 · The Build
80 pts

Creativity & Originality

40

How unique and imaginative is your take on the topic? Bold, unconventional ideas score higher than safe, predictable ones.

Technical Execution & Effort

20

Is the project well built for your age and skill level? Judges look at reliability, craft quality, and visible effort — not just complexity.

Depth & Completeness

10

Does the project feel complete and thought through? Layered features and sustained focus score better than a rushed build.

Relevance to Topic

10

Does the project connect to your chosen topic? Abstract interpretations are fine — just be able to explain the link.

Phase 02 · Live Day
20 pts

The Pitch — Presentation & Clarity

20

How clearly did you present your project? Judges want to see you understand what you built and can explain it simply. Genuine beats polished.

★   Closing Ceremony Spotlight
The Best Projects Takes the Stage
The highest-scoring teams gets their projects showcased live at TechQuest's closing ceremony — in front of the entire school, teachers, and guests.
Eligibility: All participants with a submitted project qualify automatically.
Selection: Highest grand total across both phases wins.
Tiebreaker: Phase 01 Creativity score decides.
On the day: Winner is announced at the ceremony and given 3–5 minutes to present. Their name is displayed on the event board.

A few things to know before you start building.

Open ToClasses 5 – 12
Team SizeSolo or teams of 2 – 3
TopicYour own choice — approved by event head
ApprovalGet approved by May end, then start

Topic approval. You choose your own topic. Speak to any event head by May end, share your idea, and get their go-ahead. Once approved, you can start building. This is a brief check — not a barrier.

✦  AI tools are fully allowed. Use Claude, Gemini, Lovable, ChatGPT, or any other AI to help build your site. This competition tests creativity, resource management, and understanding — not raw coding ability. You are not limited by what you currently know.

Your code is your own. Judges evaluate the final experience — not what's inside the files. How you structure or write your code is entirely up to you.

On event day, bring your best. Make sure your project is working and accessible before you arrive. Test it in advance — broken demos score poorly even with a great idea.
